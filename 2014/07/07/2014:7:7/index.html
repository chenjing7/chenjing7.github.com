Lean into the pain

看了几篇，天才少年Aaron Swartz的博客 。惊叹天才原来注定是与众不同的，看的第一篇是HOWTO: Be more productive，最有名博文是这2篇：

  • HOWTO: Be more productive
  • Who Writes Wikipedia？

不幸的是，这位天才少年26岁自杀了，感兴趣可以了解一下他的生平，简直可以拍成Hollywood电影。

因为，博客里面有很多值得我们思考的东西，我挑了一篇没有被翻译过的文章和大家分享，自己看了，蛮有收获。个人翻译水平很LOW，光标题就想了很久，最后用了“让暴风雨来得更猛烈些”。所以，有不对的地方，欢迎留言指正。

Lean into the pain

When you first begin to exercise, it's somewhat painful.
你第一次开始练习的时候，总会感到难受。
Not widely painful, like touching a hot stove/火炉/, but enough that if your goal was to avoid pain, you certainly would stop doing it.
这种难受不像平常的疼痛，比如碰到了火炉。如果你的目标就是逃避痛苦，你自然会放弃继续做下去。
But if you keep exercising...well,it keeps getting more painful.
但是，如果你继续保持练习，你会更加痛苦。
When you are done, if you've really pushed yourself, you often feel exhausted and sore.
当你真的这么做了，并且你还在强迫自己做练习，你会经常感到厌倦和恼火。
And the next morning it's even worse.
更糟糕的是，明天情况会变得更差。

If that was all that happened, you'd probably never do it.
如果所有这些都发生了，你很可能不会再继续做练习。
It's not that much fun being sore.
酸苦毕竟不是很有趣的。
Yet we do it anyway--because we know that, in the long run, the pain will make us stronger.
然而，我们还是做了--因为我们知道，从长远来看，痛苦会让我们变得更强。
Next time we will be able to run harder and lift more before the pain starts.
下次，在痛苦之前我们能跑的更快，能干更多事情。

And knowing this makes all the difference.
这是所有差异的来源。
Indeed,we come to see the pain as a sort of pleasure--it feels good to really push yourself,to fight through the pain and make yourself stronger.
事实上，我们来看看把痛苦当做一种乐趣的话，当你强迫自己的时候会感觉良好，去对抗痛苦吧，你会变得更强。
Feel the burn!
热血起来吧！
It's fun to wake up sore the next morning, because you know that's just a sign that you're getting stronger.
明天能够带着酸痛醒来是多么有趣的事啊，因为你知道这是你在不断变强的信号。

Few people realize it, but psychological pain works the same way.
很少有人意识到这一点，但是心理上的疼痛起的作用是一样的。
Most people threat psychological pain like the hot stove--if starting thinking about something scares them or stresses them out, they quickly stop thinking about it and change the subject.
很多人把心理上的痛苦当做火炉来对待--如果开始就想做这些事会让他们望而却步，他们会很快停止思考然后换个目标。

The problem is that the topics that are most painful also tend to be the topics that are most important for us: the are the projects we most want to do, the relationships we most care about, the decisions that have the biggest consequences for our future, the most dangerous risks that we run.
问题在于，最痛苦的话题往往是对我们最重要的话题：这些是我们最想做的项目，是我们最关心的联系，是那些对我们未来产生重大后果的决定，是我们冒的最大的险。
We're scared of them because we know that the stakes are so high.
我们望而却步因为我们知道需要面对的风险太大。
But if we never think about them, we can never do anything about them.
但是如过我们从来没有思考它们，我们永远不会对它们做任何事情。

Ray Dalio writes:
Ray Dalio 这样写过：

It's a fundamental law of nature to evolve one has to push one's limits,which is painful, in order to gain strength--whether it's in the form of lifting weights, facing problems, head-on, or in any other way.
自然一个基本的原则：一个人要有所发展不得不抵抗他的极限来获得力量，这是痛苦的--不管是举重的形式，面对问题，迎头而上，或者另外的形式。
Nature gave us pain as messaging device that to tell us we are approaching, or that we have exceeded/超越/, our limits in some way.
自然把痛苦当做一个信息设备给我们，告诉我们我们正接近或者在某种程度上已经超过了自身的极限。
At the same time, nature made the process of getting stronger require us to push our limits.
同样的，自然的过程就是需要我们抵抗极限来变得更强壮。
Gaining strength is the adaptation of the body and the mind to encountering/对抗/ one's limits, which is painful.
获得力量是身心对于对抗自身极限的适应，这是痛苦的。
In other words, both pain and strength typically result from encountering one's barriers/障碍/ .
换句话说，痛苦和力量通常都是对抗障碍的结果。
When we encounter pain, we are at an important juncture/时刻/ in our decision-making process.
当我们忍受疼痛，我们正在决策过程的重要关头。

Yes it's painful, but the trick is to make that mental shift.
确实，那很痛苦，但是诀窍在于思想上的转变。
To realize that the pain is not something awful to be postponed/延迟/ and avoided,but a signal that you're getting stronger--something to savor/品尝/ and enjoy.
需要意识到，痛苦不是可怕的可以延迟或者避免的事情，它是你正在变强的一个信号，需要去品尝和感受。
It's what makes you better.
那会让你感受倍增。

Pretty soon, when you start noticing something that causes you psychic/精神的/ pain, you'll get excited about it, not afraid.
不久，当你开始注意当让你精神上痛苦的事情，你会感到很激动而不是害怕它。
Ooh, anther chance to get stronger.
哦，又一个变强的机会。
You'll seek out things you're scared of and intentionally/有意的/ confront/面对/ them, because it's an easy way to get the great rewards of self=improvement.
你会寻找一些你原本害怕的事情，故意地面对它们。因为这是你得到自我巨大提升最简单的方式。
Dalio suggests thinking of each one as a puzzle, inside of which is embedded/把...嵌入/ a beautiful gem/宝石/ .
Dalio的建议是把每件东西都当做一个嵌了宝石的难题。
If you fight through the pain to solve the puzzle, you unlock it and get to keep the gem.
如果你战胜了痛苦，解决了难题，你打开它就会得到宝石。

The trick is: when you start feeling that psychological pain coming on, don't draw back from it and cower/畏缩/ --lean into it.
诀窍是：当你开始感到心理痛苦将要到来的时候，不要退缩，扑身进入它。
Lean into the pain.
让暴风雨来得更猛烈些。

In agile software development, there is a phrase: If it hurts, do it more often.
在快速软件开发，有句话：如果它伤到了你，更需要经常去做它。

For example, imagine Jane and Joan are working on a software project together.
比如，假设Jane和Joan一起在做一个软件工程，
They both have a copy of the code;Jane is making the error messages friendlier while Joan is adding new feature.
他们都有了源代码的副本。Jane做让错误信息更友好，Joan增加新的功能。
They both work on their task for days and days until it's finally done.
他们一天天的工作，最好完成了各自的任务。
Now they face a problem: they need to merge their different changes back together.
现在，他们面临一个问题：他们需要把各自不同的变化合并在一起。

Maybe you've had this problem, either with code or with text documents:
你可能遇到过这样的问题，不管是写代码还是做文本文件：
you send a draft of a report to two friends, both suggest different changes ,and you should merge all changes back into original document.
你发了一个报告的草案给两个朋友。两人有不同的修改想法，你需要把所有的不同点融合在原始的文件。
It's incredibly annoying--and doing it with software is way worse.
这令人难以忍受，做软件更是糟糕。
so people put it off.
所以很多人就束之高阁。
Jane thinks "you know, let me just make the thank you message a little nicer before we merge"
Jane觉得，合并不同点之前你只是让我把致谢提醒做的更好点。
and Joan thinks "you know, let me add one more feature before we merge".
Joan觉得，合并不同点之前你只是让我增加一些功能。

They keep putting the merge off, and every time they do the task gets bigger and more painful.
他们一直把合并的事拖着，每次他们按自己的做，合并的任务就变得越大越艰难。
But they have to do it eventually.
但是他们最终还是要去完成合并的事。
By then the merge is so big that it takes days of painstaking work just to piece together the already-written code.
到那时，他们要艰苦地工作好几天把已经写好的代码拼凑在一起。
It's an arduous/艰巨的/, painful process--which makes Joan and Jane just want to put it off even longer next time.
这是件多么艰苦的工作。所以Joan和Jane才想要把它拖着，想着下次再说吧。

The agile approach, however, is to do the opposite: merging hurts, so we will do it more often.
明智的选择恰恰是做相反的事：合并痛苦，我们将经常这么做。
Instead of merging every couple weeks, or couple months, we'll merge every single day, or every couple hour.
并不是每两周或者每两月合并一次，我们会每天甚至每两个小时合并一次。
Even if Jane and Joan aren't even close to finished with their work, they will check in what they have so far(maybe with some special code deactivating it until it's finished)
即使Jane 和 Joan 还没接近完成他们的工作，他们也能检查他们到目前为止做了什么（或许是因为特殊的代码阻止了进程）
so they don't end up in merge hell later on.
所以他们就不用到最后再来合并，痛苦简直和地狱一般。
These very small merges tend not to be painful at all, they're so easy that you hardly even notice.
这些小的合并不是特别痛苦，它们很简单有时连你自己也没察觉。

The same principle shows up all across software development:
同样的准则遍布整个软件开发行业：
from testing to releasing, your natural inclination/倾向/ is to put off painful things,
从测试到发布，你自然的倾向就是把痛苦的事情拖着，
when doing them more often actually is much easier.
但是当你经常去做的时候，实际上会更简单。

And I don't think it's limited to software.
我觉得这个准则并不限制在软件行业。
I think the same principle would work even if, for some odd reason, you were required to touch a hot stove for an hour.
我觉的同样的准则会起作用，即使由于奇怪的原因，你被要求去碰火炉一小时。
Procrastinating and putting it off until you have no choice to hold your hand to the stove for a full hour would end up being very painful.
拖延和推迟直到你没办法举起手贴着火炉整整一小时，最后手烧的很严重。
But if you did it in small frequent bits, just quick taps of the stove with your finger that eventually added up to an hour,it would't be so bad at all.
但如果你把它分成许多小片，只是快速用你的指尖碰一下火炉，这样最终加起来会有一小时，但不会伤得很严重。
Again, the trick is not to run from the pain.
再次强调下，秘诀是：不要在痛苦中逃跑。

Of all the self-improvement tricks I've learned, this one was by far the most surprising--and by far the most impactful.
所有我所学到的自我提高技巧里面，这是迄今为止效果最让你惊讶的。
I spent most of my life hemmed in by my talents.
人生的大部分���间里，我被我的天赋所束缚。
I knew I had strengths and weaknesses and it just seemed obvious I should find jobs that fit my strengths.
我知道自己的长处和短处，似乎显而易见地我应该找自己擅长的工作。
It seemed crazy to take a job probed my weaknesses.
做一份探索自我缺点的工作似乎很傻逼。

Sure, there were somethings, over there, that I wished I was better at, but they seemed so far away.
当然，我希望有一些别的东西我是擅长的，但往往只是我的异想天开。
Meanwhile, there were lots of things over here that I was good at.
与此同时，也有另外一些东西我是擅长的。
Why not just keep doing them?
为什么不继续做自己擅长的呢？
Sure, I realized intellectually/知性上/ that I could get better at the other stuff, but it hardly seemed worth the pain of trying.
确实，理性告诉我，我可以在其他方面做得更好，但是去尝试的痛苦却是不值得的。

I'd learned not to shrink from hard truths,
我明白不要在艰难面前退缩。
so I'd literally have this conversation with myself: " Yes, I know: if I got better at selling things to people[or whatever it was], I'd be much better off.
所以，我真的和自己有过交谈：“是的，我知道：如果我擅长卖东西给别人，我会越来越富裕。
But look at how painful I find selling: just thinking about it makes me want to run and hide!
但是再来看看我卖东西时候的痛苦：想想就会让我想要逃离和躲避！
Sure, it'd be great if I could do it, but is it really worth all that pain?"
确定，如果我真的能做到卖任何东西给别人，那会很伟大，但是值得我去忍受卖东西的痛苦吗？”

Now I realize this is a bogus argument: it is not the pain is so bad that it makes me flee,
现在我意识到这是虚假的：并不是痛苦如此糟糕让我逃离，
it's that the importance of the topic triggers a fight-or-flight reaction deep in my reptile brain.
而是这个它的重要性在我小脑深处触发了“非战即逃”的反应。
If instead of thinking about it as a scary subject to avoid, I think it as an exciting opportunity to get better,
我把痛苦看做一个让让人兴奋的让自己变强的机会，而不是一个可怕的事情去逃避，
then it's no longer a cost-benefit tradeoff/权衡/ at all: both sides are a benefit -- I get the benefits of being good at selling and the fun of getting better at something.
这样痛苦就不在是一个成本-收益的权衡：两边都有好处--我能在卖东西的时候得到钱，同时能享受熟能生巧的乐趣。

Do this enough times and your whole outlook on life begins to change.
这样做足够多次，你的人生前景会开始发生改变。
It's no longer a scary world, hemming you in, but an exciting one full of exciting adventures to pursue.
再也不会是一个可怕的世界困扰你，而是一个激动人心的世界，充满了奇幻的挑战让你去追寻。

Tackling something big like this is terrifying; it's far too much to start with.
应付一个大工程是可怕的，太多需要做，会让人无从下手。
It's always better to start small.
从小开始会相对容易点。
What's something you have been avoid thinking about?
哪些是你逃避思考的？
It can be anything -- a relationship difficulty, a problem at work, something at your todo list that you've been avoiding.
可以是所有的事--人际关系紧张，工作问题，在todo list上还有很多没做。
Call it to mind--despite the pain it brings--and just sort of let it sit there.
提醒自己--尽管它带来了痛苦，那就让它在那吧。
Acknowledge that thinking about it is painful and feel good about yourself for being able to do it anyway.
确实，想想就是件痛苦的事，但无论如何，你要相信自己能做好。
Feel it for becoming less painful as you force yourself to keep thinking about it.
当你强迫自己继续想时，你会渐渐感到不那么痛苦。
See, you are getting stronger!
看吧，这样你就变强了！

Ok，take a break. But when you're ready, come back to it, and start thinking of concrete things you can do about it.
Ok，说了这么多，停一下。当你准备好行动的时候，回到这里，开始想象那些具体的你能做的事。
See how it's not as scary as you thought? See how good it feels to actually do something about it?
看是不是没有你想象的那么可怕？看真正开始做的时候，是不是感觉很赞？

Next time you start feeling that feeling, that sense of pain from deep in your head that tells you to avoid a subject--ignore it.
下次当潜意识的痛苦告诉你去逃避的时候，忽略这种感觉。
Lean into the pain instead.
让暴风雨来得更猛烈些。
You'll be glad you did.
当你回头看时，你会惊讶原来你可以做那么多事。
